Projects

I like approaching big problems head on, working with motivated people on solutions that can improve lives.

The enTECH Computer Club (enTECH is a portmanteau of “engage technology”) was co-founded by Dr. Peter M. Hoang and I in Fall 2015, as a student club with the Waterloo Undergraduate Student Union (formally the Federation of Students) at the University of Waterloo. EnTECH was started to support older adults who live away from their families in using technology to keep in contact with them, for improved mental health. As news about enTECH and its work spread in the community, enTECH has been asked to support other populations that may struggle to use technology without guidance, including older adults, cancer patients, and immigrants.


MentalED is a set of curriclum documents designed to teach middle schoolers mental health concepts from the ground up, taking a biopsychosocial approach to presenting the content. I began devising mentalED when I spoke to a middle school class, who unexpectedly ended up asking me a lot of questions about mental health.

I have been a proud editor of the Chem13 News publication released by the University of Waterloo's Department of Chemistry since Fall 2014. Chem13 News is a monthly magazine for high school chemistry teachers, featuring teaching tips, best practices, and advice for this often difficult topic, and is now available online for free.
